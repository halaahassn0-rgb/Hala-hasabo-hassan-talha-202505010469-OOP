2.1 Why are the variables declared as private?

The variables are declared as private to protect the data. This is called encapsulation. It prevents direct access to the variables from outside the class.

2.2 What happens if the variables are declared public?

If the variables are public, any class can access and modify them directly. This may cause incorrect or invalid data and reduce security.

2.3 Why do we use getters and setters?

We use getters and setters to access and update private variables safely. They provide controlled access to the data and improve data security.
